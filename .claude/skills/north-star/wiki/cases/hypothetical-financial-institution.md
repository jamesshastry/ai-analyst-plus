# Hypothetical Financial Institution — "We Are Their Primary Bank"

## TL;DR
This is a **hypothetical worked example**, not a real bank. The playbook walks a fictional financial institution through the mind-mapping workflow: a messy sticky-note brainstorm refines into six clusters, then into five inputs (funding effectiveness, insights action, financial-life integration, trust/reliability, conscious acquisition) under the North Star statement "If we do all of this right, we will be their primary bank." Used to teach input refinement, not as evidence of bank practice.

## Story
The financial-institution case is the playbook's running example for chapter 3's mind-mapping workflow. The mind map deliberately starts messy: scattered sticky notes covering Savings, Direct deposit, Acquisition, "Integrated into financial life," Fast Funding, Recurring deposits, Trust, and more — the kind of unstructured brainstorm a cross-functional team produces in the first 30 minutes of an input workshop [case-study-fragment-p028-l0625-financial-institution-brainstorm-process].

The case then walks the team through refinement. The messy brainstorm is reorganized into six grouping clusters — "More related to North Star?", Funding, Reliability reputation, Integrated into financial life, Acquisition, and Understand and act — and finally into five named inputs: (1) Funding effectiveness, (2) Understanding and acting on insights, (3) Financial life integration, (4) Trust/reliability/reputation, and (5) Conscious consumer acquisition [case-study-fragment-p030-l0676-financial-institution-five-inputs].

The North Star statement that anchors the inputs is "If we do all of this right, then… we will be their primary bank," operationalized as "# of users with direct deposit activity AND spending activity AND account transfers… last 30d" [case-study-fragment-p030-l0683-financial-institution-primary-bank-nsm]. The construction is notable: the strategic intent ("primary bank") is the qualitative statement, and the measurement is an explicit boolean intersection of three behaviors over a 30-day window. The case demonstrates the playbook's pattern of pairing a one-sentence aspirational statement with a precise operational definition.

Because the case is hypothetical, the playbook does not document outcomes, team structure, or evolution beyond the brainstorm → refinement progression itself.

## Why it works
The case is the playbook's clearest worked example of input refinement as a craft activity. It shows that the gap between a messy brainstorm and a usable input list is real work, not magic, and it shows that the right unit of refinement is grouping clusters first, named inputs second. The NSM operationalization (AND-of-three-behaviors over 30d) also demonstrates how to translate an aspirational statement into a measurable construction without losing the strategic intent.

## What broke
The case is explicitly hypothetical — no real financial institution is named, and the labels on the mind map are illustrative. No outcome data exists to validate. Confidence is medium because there is nothing external to verify against; the case is pedagogical. Curators should never treat the input list or NSM operationalization as evidence of how any actual bank operates.
