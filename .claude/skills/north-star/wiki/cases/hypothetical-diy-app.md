# Hypothetical DIY Project App — "Inspire and Equip DIY-Curious Customers"

## TL;DR
This is a **hypothetical worked example**, not a real app. The playbook fills in a four-tier statement worksheet for a DIY-project app: the North Star statement is "Inspire and equip new DIY-curious customers to finish their projects," measured as Completed Projects Shared (CPS) or Weekly New Project Completed (WNPC), with three inputs (match users to projects, provide guidance, motivate via community) and concrete opportunities and interventions under each.

## Story
The DIY-project app is the playbook's filled-in worked example for the four-tier statement exercise on p. 21. It models how a team moves from a one-sentence North Star statement down to specific, this-quarter interventions through two intermediate layers — inputs and opportunities.

**Top tier — the North Star statement and measurement.** The statement is "Inspire and equip new DIY-curious customers to finish their projects!", with two candidate measurements: Completed Projects Shared (CPS) and Weekly New Project Completed (WNPC) [case-study-fragment-p021-l0483-diy-app-north-star-statement]. The statement is structured the way the playbook teaches: a verb-driven sentence about a specific user segment ("DIY-curious") that names the outcome behavior ("finish their projects").

**Second tier — three inputs.** The NSM is driven by three named inputs: (1) Match users with appropriate projects, (2) Provide guidance and support, (3) Motivate and inspire by connecting with community [case-study-fragment-p021-l0485-diy-app-three-inputs]. The three split cleanly into discovery (match), execution (guidance), and social reinforcement (community) — the three things a DIY-curious user needs to actually finish a project.

**Third and fourth tiers — opportunities and interventions.** Each input is expanded into specific opportunities (better understand interests+intent, expand project selection, collect more problem data, self-service help, expert guidance, increase community size, connect like-minded DIY-ers) and concrete interventions (capture goals at signup, SEO, YouTube intent ads, expert on-call experiment, revised project, FAQ IA, project followers, experiments, kudos) [case-study-fragment-p021-l0487-diy-app-opportunities-interventions]. The bottom tier is sprint-scale and concrete; the top tier is years-scale and aspirational.

The case is the playbook's only fully filled-in instance of all four tiers at once, which is why it appears as the figure on p. 21 rather than as a prose narrative.

## Why it works
The case demonstrates the framework's most distinctive artifact: a single page that links a one-sentence aspirational statement to a sprint-week intervention with two intermediate layers. The discipline the worksheet enforces is that every intervention must trace up through an input to the NSM — there is no place to park "interesting work" that doesn't ladder. The three-input split (discovery / execution / social) is also a reusable pattern for any "help users complete a complex behavior" product.

## What broke
The case is explicitly hypothetical — no real DIY app is named, and the opportunities/interventions are illustrative, not validated. Confidence is medium because there is nothing external to verify; the case stands as a worksheet template, not as evidence about any product. The two measurement candidates (CPS and WNPC) are offered side by side without the playbook resolving which is preferred — appropriate for a teaching artifact, but curators should not surface this case as evidence for either measurement choice.
