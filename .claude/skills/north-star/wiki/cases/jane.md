# Jane — From OKR Theater to Real Empowerment

## TL;DR
Jane is a narrative case (anonymized senior engineer) used in chapter 7 to show how a 60-minute cross-functional NSM workshop can break a team out of OKR-theater quarterly planning. After the session, her team explored foundational bets in 30 minutes and produced NSM candidates in 60. Months later, Jane reported the team no longer felt like they were "making stuff up."

## Story
Jane is the playbook's closing narrative case. She is a senior engineer whose team was in the middle of quarterly planning under a VP of product who "spent their last meeting talking about empowerment, OKRs, and the importance of goal setting." Jane was frustrated — empowerment, in her org, had been reduced to "letting us do what we want," and the team felt it was "just spinning our wheels" [case-study-fragment-p061-l1416-jane-frustration-context].

The intervention was deliberately small. The authors arranged a short session with Jane, the VP of product, the team's PM, a designer, and a data scientist from a different product team inside the company — explicitly chosen as a neutral third party. Within 30 minutes the group was exploring foundational company bets; within 60 minutes they had a couple of NSM candidates, skipping freely around the workshop format rather than running it strictly [case-study-fragment-p061-l1428-jane-workshop-intervention].

Months later, the authors checked back in. Things weren't perfect, but they were better. Jane no longer felt like the team was "making stuff up." Some features they shipped didn't have the intended impact, but the team learned important insights about what customers valued, and Jane was starting to feel "real empowerment" — not the OKR-theater version, but the kind that comes from knowing what the team is trying to move and why [case-study-fragment-p061-l1438-jane-outcome-real-empowerment].

The case closes the playbook by anchoring the framework in an organizational dysfunction (empowerment-as-permission) that the NSF is positioned to repair.

## Why it works
The Jane case demonstrates two operational claims the playbook makes elsewhere: (1) the NSM workshop is a fast intervention — a useful first cut can come out of one 60-minute cross-functional session, not a quarter-long roadmap exercise; and (2) the framework's contribution to "empowerment" is not autonomy in the abstract, it's giving teams a shared metric and a shared theory of value so that autonomy has a direction. The neutral-third-party detail (the outside data scientist) is the playbook's quiet endorsement of facilitation as a workshop-design choice.

## What broke
The case is anonymized by design — neither Jane nor her company is named — so there is no path to external verification. The outcome is reported qualitatively ("better, not perfect") rather than quantitatively, and the specific NSM candidates the workshop produced are not stated. These are appropriate for a closing-narrative case but cap confidence at medium and preclude the case from being used as evidence for any specific NSM construction.
