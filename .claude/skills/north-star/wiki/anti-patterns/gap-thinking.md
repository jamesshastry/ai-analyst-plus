# Gap thinking causing momentum loss

## TL;DR
When teams adopt the framework with a gap-thinking model — fix a future end-state, plan to close the gap — they lose momentum because the endpoint never arrives and the present gets devalued. Jabe Bloom's remediation is *present thinking*: recognize the reality of the present and improve it incrementally.

## Spot signals
- Long planning cycles with infrequent learning checkpoints.
- The team's vocabulary is dominated by "future state," "target state," "end goal."
- Energy drops as the gap to the end-state proves stubborn.
- Teams "lose steam and give up" when the gap doesn't close on schedule.

## Fix recipe
1. Shift to **present thinking** (p. 46-47, Jabe Bloom) — recognize the reality of the present and work to change it incrementally.
2. Use the present-thinking questions: Where are we? What do I need now? How do I improve the current way of working?
3. Apply the playbook's NSM-adapted versions (p. 47): Can we align current efforts with chosen inputs? What decisions do we need to make today? Can every team member trace work to the NSM?
4. Replace one-jump diagrams with stair-step loops (Fig. p. 46): each loop pairs a current condition with an ideal current condition.

## Examples
- **Contested:** Some practitioners argue gap and present thinking are complementary, not a dichotomy — strategy needs a future state, execution needs present focus. See debate page.
