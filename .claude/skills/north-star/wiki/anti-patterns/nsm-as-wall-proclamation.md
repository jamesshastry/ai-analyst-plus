# NSM as a "nail it to the wall" proclamation

## TL;DR
Announcing an NSM and expecting teams to follow it without putting systems behind it produces a flavor-of-the-week outcome. The framework decays as soon as attention shifts. Ted Clark: "Your North Star is not a proclamation you nail to the wall and magically everyone starts to follow."

## Spot signals
- NSM is announced once at an all-hands but not referenced in subsequent operational meetings.
- No designated sponsor with influence and authority owns the NSM.
- No change-management plan to socialize it across the org.
- New hires don't learn about the NSM during onboarding.
- Approval / planning processes don't reference the NSM.

## Fix recipe
1. Designate a sponsor with both influence and authority.
2. Get explicit leadership buy-in tied to operational rituals.
3. Run communication and change-management processes to socialize the concept across all orgs.
4. Build an onboarding process that introduces the framework to new team members.
5. Wire NSM checks into approval/planning processes.

## Examples
- **Amplitude (p. 44):** Weekly NSM reporting, NSM cited at quarterly all-hands alongside pipeline/revenue, customer health usage tracked in QBRs.
- Inverse: [Current dysfunction stops progress](current-dysfunction-stops-progress.md) — when teams stall *because* they correctly notice no systems exist.
