# Rushing the North Star statement exercise

## TL;DR
Filling in the four-tier statement worksheet quickly, as a box-checking exercise, produces a shallow statement and skips the team alignment work the exercise exists to surface. John Cutler runs entire workshops just chipping away at this statement — if your team allots 15 minutes, you aren't doing the exercise.

## Spot signals
- Workshop schedule allots minimal time to the statement (e.g., 15 minutes).
- The team fills in boxes without arguing over wording.
- The statement reads like a template, not like a position the team has taken.
- The team can't qualitatively explain the framework to outsiders even after the workshop.

## Fix recipe
1. Allocate dedicated time — John Cutler runs full workshops just on the statement.
2. Force qualitative alignment before quantitative: "if someone can't explain the North Star Framework qualitatively, they won't be able to explain it quantitatively" (p. 19).
3. Encourage introspection and explicit disagreement during the exercise — disagreement IS the signal of work happening.
4. Iterate: complete the worksheet, then revise as a team; expect multiple passes.

## Examples
- Opposite trap: [Cycle of doubt paralysis](cycle-of-doubt-paralysis.md) — over-iterating without ever committing. The two anti-patterns bracket the same axis: rushing under-invests, paralysis never lands.
