# "It'll never work here" — letting dysfunction stop progress

## TL;DR
Teams sometimes abandon NSM adoption because they perceive their company's existing problems — vanity-metric culture, feature factory, tech-stack organization — as too large to overcome. But those imperfections don't have to block progress. Start where you are; start small.

## Spot signals
- Workshop energy collapses mid-session as participants enumerate organizational obstacles.
- The team concludes the framework "can't work here" because leadership is feature-focused, metrics are vanity, org is around tech stack, etc.
- Adoption stalls because the perfect implementation requires changes outside the team's authority.

## Fix recipe
1. **Start where you are** (p. 42 strategy): acknowledge current imperfections but don't let them stop progress; begin by adopting the framework's *language* and slowly chipping away.
2. **Start small** (p. 42 strategy): pick a product scope you can impact — even a small percentage of the business — and build/test an NSM for just that scope.
3. Map current prescriptive work, A/B tests, and KPIs to the strategy you already have, even if messy.
4. Frame the work as continuous learning (Chapter 5 'making it stick'), not a one-time transformation.
